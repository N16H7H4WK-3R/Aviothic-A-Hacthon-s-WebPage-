Aviothic 1.0 Code of Conduct

Introduction

Welcome to Aviothic 1.0! This is a 12-hour hackathon designed to encourage creativity, collaboration, and innovation in the tech community. To ensure a safe and inclusive environment for all participants, we ask that you abide by the following Code of Conduct.

Team Requirements

Each team must consist of a minimum of 2 members and a maximum of 4 members. No individual submissions will be accepted.

General Rules

  1. Be respectful and courteous to all participants, organizers, sponsors, and volunteers.
  2. Harassment and discrimination of any kind will not be tolerated. This includes, but is not limited to, any form of verbal, physical, or sexual abuse, as well as offensive or derogatory language.
  3. Do not engage in any form of cheating or plagiarism. Your submission must be your original work.
  4. Use only legal software and hardware for your projects.
  5. Use of illegal substances or alcohol is strictly prohibited during the hackathon.
  6. Respect the venue and equipment provided to you. Any damage to property will result in disqualification as well as fine.
  7. Ensure that your project and presentation are appropriate for all ages.
  8. By participating in the hackathon, you agree to be photographed and/or filmed for promotional purposes.
  9. Work collaboratively and in a respectful manner with your team members. This includes giving credit where credit is due and respecting the ideas and opinions of others

Reporting Guidelines

If you witness or experience any violations of this Code of Conduct, please report it immediately to one of the event organizers. All reports will be kept confidential.

Consequences of Violations

If any participant violates this Code of Conduct, the organizers may take any action they deem appropriate, including but not limited to, expulsion from the hackathon and disqualification from the competition.

Conclusion

We hope that all participants will have a fun and productive experience at Aviothic 1.0. Let's work together to create an environment that is inclusive, welcoming, and respectful for all.
